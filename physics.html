Physics of Underwater Imaging

The physics governing underwater imaging involves several critical concepts, crucial for both capturing and enhancing images:

  • Snell's Law: Refraction affects how we perceive underwater objects. Snell's Law describes this phenomenon as \( n_1 \sin \theta_1 = n_2 \sin \theta_2 \)
  • Beer-Lambert Law: This law describes how light absorption and scattering work: \( I = I_0 e^{-\alpha x} \), where \( I_0 \) is the initial intensity, \( \alpha \) is the absorption coefficient, and \( x \) is the distance.
  • Light Attenuation: The intensity of light decreases exponentially with depth due to absorption and scattering. This concept can be quantified as: \( I(z) = I_0 \cdot e^{-kz} \), where \( I_0 \) is the initial intensity, \( k \) is the attenuation coefficient, and \( z \) is the depth.
