Introduction

The world beneath the surface of the water bodies is a realm of visual wonder filled with vibrant marine life, mesmerizing landscapes, and intricate ecosystems. Yet, capturing this beauty through photography or videography poses unique challenges such as reduced visibility, altered colour spectrums, and distorted perspectives. These factors often result in images that are hazy, monochromatic, or lacking in detail.

This is where the field of Underwater Image Enhancement comes into play. It is a specialized domain within the broader area of image processing and computer vision, specifically focused on improving the visual quality of underwater images and videos. These improvements can range from basic adjustments, like altering brightness and contrast, to complex manipulations aimed at colour correction, reducing blurriness, and mitigating the effects of particle scattering in the water.

The ultimate goal of underwater image enhancement is not just to produce aesthetically pleasing visuals but also to aid in scientific research, underwater exploration, and environmental monitoring. By improving the clarity and fidelity of underwater images, researchers and scientists can gather more accurate data, which in turn contributes to our understanding and conservation of aquatic ecosystems.
